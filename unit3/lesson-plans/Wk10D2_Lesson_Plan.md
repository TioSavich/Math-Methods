# Week 10, Day 2 - Multiplication and Division Operation Sense

## Course Information
- **Course:** E343 Math Education Methods for Pre-Service Teachers
- **Institution:** Indiana University
- **Class Duration:** ~75 minutes

## Goals

1. Understand the transition in number sense and computation standards from 2nd to 3rd grade
2. Identify the five multiplicative problem structures (equal groups, comparison, array, area, combination)
3. Distinguish between sharing (partition) and measurement (quotative) division by varying the unknown in multiplicative problems
4. Understand the commutative and associative properties of multiplication

## Materials

- Unit 3 Course Packet (pp. 15-20)
- Vertical articulation documents for grades 2-3 (Canvas)
- Printed problem sort cards (Slide 8 problems)
- Large post-it paper and markers
- PowerPoint: Wk10D2_MultDivOpSense

## Lesson Outline

### Opening / Quick Follow-Ups (5 min)

Review semantic vs. calculation equations (Slide 3). In "__ + 25 = 40," the blank follows the story. In "40 - 25 = __," the blank is isolated for computation. Quick check with students: do they remember this distinction from the previous session? Post process standards as reference.

### Vertical Articulation: 2nd-3rd Grade Standards (25 min)

#### Round 1: Number Sense (12 min)

- Assign groups to read the Number Sense strand of the 2nd-3rd grade vertical articulation document (Canvas).
- Groups complete Course Packet p. 15: main ideas, what's new in 3rd grade, what's dropped from 2nd grade, and standards they don't understand.
- Key points to surface in discussion:
  - Fractions are introduced in 3rd grade
  - Place value range expands significantly
  - Rounding is introduced

#### Round 2: Computation & Algebraic Thinking (13 min)

- Same process for Computation & Algebraic Thinking strand, using Course Packet p. 16.
- Key points to surface:
  - Multiplication and division are introduced in 3rd grade
  - Addition/subtraction fluency within 100 is assumed
  - Two-step word problems appear
  - Properties of operations become explicit
- If time permits, ask students to compare across strands: What is number sense about versus what is computation about?

### Transition: What Does Operation Sense Mean? (5 min)

Display Slide 6. Give students 2 minutes to discuss in groups: What does multiplication mean? What does division mean? Collect responses. Establish that operation sense means knowing what an operation means without performing a specific computation. Frame the next activity: problem structures give different ways of making sense of what multiplication and division mean.

### Multiplication & Division Problem Structures - First Pass (10 min)

**Part A Problem Sort** (Slide 8, Course Packet)

- Hand out 5 printed problems. Students make a representation for each and identify its structure.
- The five types:
  - Equal Groups
  - Comparison
  - Array
  - Area
  - Combination
- Debrief using Slides 17-21 as needed. Highlight: in all five problems, the product is the unknown. All are multiplication.

### Multiplication & Division Problem Structures - Second Pass (25 min)

#### Part B: Unknown Parts and Equations (15 min)

- Students work Course Packet Part B. For each problem, identify: problem type, what is known/unknown, and write a multiplication equation.
- Remind students of the N101 distinction (Slides 11-12):
  - Sharing (partition) division: number of groups known, group size unknown
  - Measurement (quotative) division: group size known, number of groups unknown
- Walk through using Slide 14 as answer key. Emphasize VdW convention: write as multiplication with the unknown in the appropriate position (e.g., __ × 5 = 30), not as division.
- Discuss the area problem at the end: because length and width are interchangeable, there are not two distinct division types for area (unlike equal groups and comparison).

#### Part C: Modeling Problems (10 min)

- Students choose 4 problems from Part B and create a representation appropriate to the problem structure:
  - Arrays for array problems
  - Equal sets for equal groups
  - Bar diagrams for comparison
  - Grid for area
- Follow VdW convention for symbolizing (p. 175): "The U.S. convention is that 4 × 8 refers to four sets of eight."

### Properties of Multiplication (if time, ~10 min)

- **Commutative property:** Arrays make this visible. 3 rows of 5 = 5 rows of 3. Physically turn the array 90 degrees.
- **Associative property:** When multiplying three numbers, grouping doesn't matter. Useful for mental math.
- Have students work the commutative/associative problems on the handout.
- Note: Distributive property and problem-solving sections are on the handout and slides but likely will not be covered today. Students can work on them independently.

### Closing (5 min)

Summarize: Today connected standards to content (2nd-3rd grade transition) and built operation sense through problem structures that vary the type and the unknown.

## Differentiation & Notes

- Students who finish Part B quickly can move to Part C or begin properties problems.
- If vertical articulation runs long, shorten to one round and assign the second as homework.
- "Make Your Own Problem" activity (Slide 15) is available as an extension for early-finishing groups.
- **Common confusion:** Students often conflate sharing and measurement division. Use concrete examples with small numbers (e.g., "12 cookies in 3 bags" vs. "12 cookies, 4 per bag").
