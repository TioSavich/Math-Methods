# Week 9, Day 1 - Indiana Standards and Math Process Standards

## Course Information
- **Course:** E343 Math Education Methods for Pre-Service Teachers
- **Institution:** Indiana University
- **Class Duration:** ~75 minutes

## Goals

1. Distinguish standards, curriculum, and the Five Practices/lesson structure
2. Understand K-2 Indiana Academic Standards for number sense, computation, and algebraic thinking
3. Understand math process standards and their role in instruction
4. Identify teacher actions and questions that support each process standard

## Materials

- Unit 3 Course Packet (pp. 1-14)
- Indiana Academic Standards documents (Canvas)
- Vertical articulation documents for K-2 (Canvas)
- Large post-it paper and markers
- PowerPoint: Wk09D1_Standards_Practices

## Lesson Outline

### Opening: Standards, Curriculum, and Lesson Structure (5-10 min)

Display Slide 3. Ask students: What's the difference between standards and curriculum? What's the Five Practices framework?

Establish three layers:
- **Standards:** Grade-level learning targets (Indiana Academic Standards)
- **Curriculum:** Scope, sequence, materials, and activities that teach toward standards
- **Lesson Structure:** The Five Practices (anticipating, monitoring, selecting, sequencing, connecting) and deliberate design that makes standards-aligned instruction happen

Clarify that today's focus is on the first two: what are K-2 standards, and what mathematical thinking do process standards demand?

### Group Formation and Task Assignment (5 min)

Divide class into six groups. Assign:
- **Groups 1-3:** One K-2 content strand each (Number Sense, Computation & Algebraic Thinking, Measurement & Data)
- **Groups 4-6:** One process standard each (Problem Solving, Reasoning & Proof, Communication)

### Activity 1: Vertical Articulation of Number Sense (K-2) (20 min)

Groups 1-3 work on content strands using the K-2 vertical articulation document and Course Packet.

**Task for each content group:**
- Read your assigned strand (K through 2nd grade)
- Complete Course Packet p. 1-2 (if Group 1: Number Sense; if Group 2: Computation & Algebraic Thinking; if Group 3: Measurement & Data)
- Document:
  - Main ideas within your strand (what is it fundamentally about?)
  - What's new in 1st grade? What's new in 2nd grade?
  - What disappears or becomes less central?
  - Which standards are confusing or need clarification?

**Key points to surface in debriefing:**
- Number sense begins with counting, subitizing, and cardinality in K; expands to place value and comparison by 2nd
- Computation moves from informal strategies (K-1) to fluency expectations (2nd)
- Algebraic thinking starts with patterns and comparison; builds toward basic equations

Allow 10 minutes for group work, then 10 minutes for whole-group reporting (2-3 min per group).

### Activity 2: Vertical Articulation of Computation and Algebraic Thinking (K-2) (20 min)

Same structure as Activity 1, but focus is on identifying:
- Which operations are introduced when
- Fluency expectations at each grade
- How unknowns move from concrete (missing addend) to more abstract (variable in equations)
- Connection between addition/subtraction and later multiplication/division

**Key points to surface:**
- Addition/subtraction with small numbers (within 10) start in K and expand to within 20 by 1st
- Fluency within 10 is expected by end of 1st; fluency within 20 by end of 2nd
- Multiplication and division do NOT appear in K-2 standards; 3rd grade is the entry point
- Algebraic thinking in K-2 is concrete: finding missing addends, recognizing patterns, understanding equal sign as "the same as" (not "answer coming up")

### Activity 3: Process Standards in Action (20 min)

Groups 4-6 work on process standards.

**Task for each process standard group:**
- Pick your process standard (Problem Solving, Reasoning & Proof, or Communication)
- Think about what this standard looks like in K-2 classrooms
- Brainstorm and list:
  - 3-4 specific teacher actions that elicit this standard
  - 3-4 specific teacher questions that push students to engage in this standard
  - An example moment (mini-scenario) where you'd use one of these questions/actions

Examples:
- **Problem Solving:** Teacher might ask "Can you solve this a different way?" or "What happens if we change the number?" Actions: give time, accept multiple strategies
- **Reasoning & Proof:** Teacher might ask "How do you know?" or "Can you prove that?" Actions: press for justification, model reasoning language
- **Communication:** Teacher might ask "Tell us what you did" or "How would you explain that to someone else?" Actions: orchestrate peer explanation, intentionally name strategies

Record on post-its. Share out in final 5 minutes (2 min per group).

### Closing (5 min)

Summarize the three layers: standards tell us what to teach, curriculum is the structure we use to teach it, and lesson design (Five Practices) is how we make that instruction count. Process standards run through everything—they're not separate content, they're how students engage with content.

## Differentiation & Notes

- If a group finishes early on vertical articulation, ask: "What would a standard look like in 3rd grade?" (preview)
- For process standards groups, if discussion stalls, provide a specific K-2 math moment: "A teacher is teaching 5 + __ = 8. What would a Problem Solving question sound like here?"
- If time runs short, assign one process standard round to pairs and debrief briefly
